# The Use of Technology in Education

---

## Introduction

---

With the increase in technological advances, especially in the recent years, technology is already transforming education globally, and Singapore is no exception. 

For example, platforms like SLS and Google classroom are starting to be used more and more.

HOWEVER, there are still a multitude of issues surrounding this. 

---

## Issues arount using technology for education

---

### Accesssibility

Despite what you may think, Singapore, like many places around the world, has a digital divide, where many households , usually those in poverty, are unable to access technology. 

However, the government and some non profits have put in effort to get these children access to laptops.

![width:1000px](imdadigitalaccess.png)

---

### Effectiveness

Although technology has many benefits, like ease of access to a lot of information, and AI chatbots being able to help with studies, it is also harmful when not used properly. 

Technology in education can be a big distraction to students, and an over-reliance on technology, which can cause major problems. 

While the government have tried to battle the distraction part, it has caused other problems too. 



![width:600px](mobileguardianhack.png)

---

---

### Privacy Concerns
With technology comes big risks, including data breaches, hacks, and more. 

Additionally, with the rise of AI, both learning tools (like SLS) and students are beginning to use AI chatbots more and more in their education. 

This can introduce issues like AI bias, and makes privacy a concern for the government.

## Perspectives of Stakeholders

### Students
- **Benefits:** Flexibility, global resources.
- **Challenges:** Increased screen time, distractions.
- **Example:** Student testimonials on tech-based learning.

### Teachers
- **Benefits:** Real-time assessments, teaching aids.
- **Challenges:** Training needs, balancing methods.

### Policymakers
- Bridging digital divides with affordable devices.
- Promoting digital literacy.
- **Example:** National programs addressing technology gaps.
